Why Flowers?

Because I love them. As far as I can remember, even as a little kid I loved flowers. The vivid colors, the complex textures, and the calming scents, everything about them fills me with joy. I love the look on people’s faces when they receive flowers. It’s such a sweet and simple way of making people feel loved.

I think starting my own flower business was obvious to most people in my life, except me. Over the years I’ve done the flowers for friends and family for birthdays, bridal showers, baby showers, bachelorettes, holiday tablescapes… you name it. I always saw them as a way to scratch my creative itch. I worked in very stressful corporate jobs for the last 10-15 years and I needed something more. I was tired of the unfulfilling grind, then our cat Nola got cancer.

She was only 6 years old. Nola got very sick very quickly and she eventually went blind. There wasn’t much she could enjoy, but she loved spending time outside in nature. I watched my blind cat courageously try to walk down stairs and navigate life. I watched her bump into walls and furniture like a Roomba. None of that stopped her from wanting to spend every last second doing what she loved, being outside.

It made me think about my own life. What was something I loved so much that I would want to spend my last free moments doing it? Playing with flowers. Growing flowers. Being surrounded by flowers. So here I am, trying to share my love of flowers and hopefully helping other people feel loved along the way.

What’s one thing you hope every person who receives your flowers feels—if they remember nothing else?

I want them to feel like they matter to someone. Someone in their life was thinking about them when they ordered the flowers. Someone was thinking about their smile while waiting for the flowers to get delivered. Someone was thinking about them and hoping they helped their day get a little bit brighter.

What’s something you’ve learned about yourself through flowers?

I’ve learned not to get too attached. I can create something magical, and it may wither away in a week. So I enjoy things as they are, while I have them. Flowers are a good metaphor for life.

If this resonates with you, consider turning that appreciation into kindness. Donations to No Kill animal charities help give vulnerable animals the time, care, and chance they deserve—so their stories don’t end too soon.
